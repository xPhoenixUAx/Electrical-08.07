FAQ

Frequently asked questions about our services and process.

Is VoltLink an electrical contractor?

No. VoltLink is a free matching service. Independent providers perform all electrical work.

Do you guarantee provider availability?

No. Availability depends on location, timing, service type, provider schedules, and local coverage.

Who verifies license and insurance?

The homeowner must verify that any hired provider carries required license and insurance.

What if the issue is dangerous right now?

For smoke, fire, active arcing, shock risk, or immediate danger, contact emergency services or the utility first.

Does VoltLink set the price for electrical work?

No. Pricing, estimates, deposits, trip fees, labor, parts, and payment terms are handled directly between the homeowner and the independent provider.

What details should I include in a request?

Share the service type, room or area, ZIP code, timing, symptoms, panel or fixture details, access notes, and whether the issue is active now.

Can I request help for a planned project?

Yes. You can submit non-urgent requests for lighting, outlets, appliance circuits, panel planning, remodel wiring, and safety review before a project starts.

Should I upload or share photos with a provider?

Photos can help providers understand the panel, fixture, outlet, access area, damage, or visible symptoms before discussing next steps.

Who handles permits and inspections?

Ask the provider before hiring. Permit requirements vary by location and scope, and the homeowner should confirm who is responsible for permits and required inspections.

Does this site warranty the work?

No. This site does not warrant or guarantee work performed by independent providers. Discuss workmanship, parts, warranty terms, and exclusions directly with the provider.

Aggregator clarity

What VoltLink does, and what remains between you and the provider.

A matching site should be clear about its role. We help organize and route requests, while independent local providers handle estimates, licensing, scheduling, permits, and the actual electrical work.

We help route the request

  • Collect the service category, urgency, property area, and project notes.
  • Help connect homeowners with available independent local providers.
  • Keep safety reminders visible before the homeowner chooses who to hire.

We do not perform the work

  • We are not an electrical contractor and do not supervise provider work.
  • We do not guarantee response time, pricing, workmanship, permits, or outcomes.
  • The homeowner must verify license and insurance before hiring.
